What is Turbidity?

Turbidity is the cloudiness of a liquid caused by suspended solids. In milk analysis, it helps detect impurities such as turmeric, coffee powder, or water dilution.

  • > 2500: Clear Milk (No contamination)
  • <= 2500: Contaminated Milk (Adulterated or diluted)

Clean milk has high turbidity due to purity and consistency. Adulterated milk drops the value.
